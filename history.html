History of Christmas and the Celebrations That Influenced it

Christmas, as it relates to Christianity, goes back to at least 300CE. However, that is the religious component. As time passed and the celebration developed, tradtions from many pagan religions were attached to it. The time leading up to Christmas, Advent, incorporated Saturnalian traditions in Italy during the Middle Ages. In the High Middle Ages, traditions from both Saturnalia and Yule were incorporated, which included carolling and the Yule boar.

Saturnalia

Santurnalia was a Roman festival in honor of Saturn, the top Roman god. It was held from the 17th of December, on the Julian calendar, and extended to the 23rd of December. This festival was celebrated with a sacrifice at the Temple of Saturn, and a public feast. This was followed by gift-giving and continual partying.

Yule

Yule was a pagan religious festival celebrated by Germanic peoples, before it was assimilated by Christianity and became part of the Christmas celebrations. Similar terms for Yule have been found in Norse traditions, and the god Odin is associated with Yule.

Fun Historical Facts

  • Christmas has assimilated so many pagan or non-christian traditions into Christmas that the total input of the religion is less than that of the pagan and non-christian traditions.
  • Jesus' birth date was most likely chosen to be in December to coincide with the winter solstice, and so draw pagans into the religion.
  • Jesus Christ was most likely born some time in June of the Gregorian calendar.
